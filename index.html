About Us

 

We are Tacton, a student-led deep-tech startup dedicated to solving one of the ocean's harshest challenges — pressure. Guided by Dr. Carl Heine and supported by university researchers, our team blends material science and engineering to create solutions for extreme underwater environments. We aim to empower next-generation ocean exploration by designing affordable, pressure-resistant materials for deep-sea vessels. Our mission is simple: To make deep-sea exploration accessible and sustainable.


Our Solution

 

At the core of our innovation is Liptatic Foam, a patent-pending composite infused with synthetic lipids. This material has been engineered to maintain structural integrity under pressures exceeding 6,000 PSI, making it ideal for deep-sea applications. Tacton's foam combines lightweight flexibility with remarkable pressure resistance, offering an efficient alternative to heavy, costly pressure shells. Validated through simulation and real-world tests at NYU and Harvard research labs, our product is built to last — from submersible drones to oceanic data buoys. We're not just innovating for today — we're laying the foundation for tomorrow's deep-sea technology.
